From Survivor to Founder

"April 3rd, 2020. Library parking lot, broad daylight. The knife went in near my heart. 45 minutes until paramedics arrived. CO₂ bubbles in my aortic artery. Emergency surgery. I survived."

Twelve years of lived experience. Homelessness. Jail. Heroin and the F-word. That stabbing could have been my end. Instead, it became my beginning.

I didn't get clean through rehab or 12-steps. I rebuilt my dopamine system through daily discipline and verification of my humanity. No withdrawals. No powerless surrender. Just progress, accountability, and purpose.

Now I'm teaching others to do the same. Because lived experience builds trust in ways that medical degrees never can.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why call it the "F-word"?
To reduce stigma and keep our platform safe. We're talking about the same thing—just with more dignity.
How are the top 10 Recovery Squad members chosen?
Based on two clear criteria: verified personal progress and helping the most people quit the F-word. Transparent scoring with opt-in public scoreboard.
What gets published about me?
Nothing unless you choose it. Everything is private by default. You control what's shared, when, and how.
Is this medical treatment?
No. We provide support, incentives, and community. We don't give medical advice or treatment. Always consult healthcare providers for medical decisions.
Where do the incentives come from?
Sponsors who want to support recovery and create positive impact. We publish transparent reports showing exactly how funds are used.
What if I relapse?
Progress over perfection. You're not out. We're here to support you through setbacks, not judge you for them.
How much does this cost?
The pilot program is completely free. We're funded by sponsors who believe in this mission.

Supriety™ is a privacy-first recovery and impact platform. We do not provide medical advice or treatment. If you're in danger, call emergency services immediately.
